Sutton Tower is a 67-story residential skyscraper located at 430 East 58th Street in the Sutton Place section of Midtown East. The building was designed by Thomas Juul-Hansen and developed by Gamma Real Estate and JVP Management. It was completed in 2022 and has 120 condominium residences ranging in size from one to four bedrooms.

The building's exterior is clad in Bavarian limestone and features a dramatic double-height lobby with a custom-carved marble reception desk. The residences feature open floor plans, extra-wide European white oak flooring, kitchens with Italian-crafted cabinetry and Sub-Zero and Wolf appliances, and primary baths with heated herringbone-patterned floors and Waterworks fittings.

Sutton Tower offers a wide range of amenities, including an attended double-height lobby and ground-level courtyard, an indoor saltwater pool, a spa suite, a fitness center with Pilates room, a regulation squash court with basketball hoop, a music practice room with piano, keyboard, guitars, and drum set, a childrenâ€™s playroom, an entertainment lounge, a sunken terrace at basement level, and a rooftop terrace with outdoor cinema, outdoor kitchen, lounge seating, and stunning views of the Manhattan skyline and the Hudson River.

Sutton Tower is located in a prime location in Sutton Place, close to Central Park, the East River, and many popular shops, restaurants, and cultural attractions. The building is a perfect choice for those who are looking for a luxurious and convenient home in the heart of Manhattan.
